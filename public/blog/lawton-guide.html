Blood Donation in Lawton: Overview

Lawton's Blood Donor Center on NW Gore Blvd serves southwestern Oklahoma's largest city and the Fort Sill military community. Active-duty service members, veterans, and civilians all contribute to Lawton's blood supply, supporting nearby communities like Cache, Elgin, and Medicine Park.

Comanche County Memorial Hospital and Southwestern Medical Center depend on Lawton-area donors. The Fort Sill military installation also creates additional demand, as service members and their families require reliable access to blood products.

Whether you are a first-time donor or a regular contributor, this guide covers everything you need to know about donating blood in Lawton in 2026, including the types of donations available, eligibility requirements, step-by-step instructions, and details about compensation.

Step-by-Step: Your Donation Visit

Here is exactly what to expect when you visit the Lawton Blood Donor Center at 1810 NW Gore Blvd:

1

Schedule Your Appointment

Book online at obi.org or call (580) 357-4450. Walk-ins are welcome at the Lawton center, but appointments get priority and reduce your wait time. Choose a time when you are well-rested and well-hydrated.

2

Check In & Registration

Arrive at 1810 NW Gore Blvd with your government-issued photo ID. First-time donors will complete a registration form with basic personal and health information. Returning donors can check in quickly with their donor ID.

3

Health Screening

A staff member will check your temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels. You will answer a confidential health questionnaire. This screening takes about 10-15 minutes and ensures your safety during the donation.

4

The Donation

You will recline in a comfortable chair while a trained phlebotomist draws your blood. Whole blood takes about 8-10 minutes. Plasma and platelet donations take longer (45 min to 2.5 hours) as blood is processed through an apheresis machine. Most donors read, watch TV, or browse their phone during the draw.

5

Rest & Refreshments

After your donation, relax for 10-15 minutes in the refreshment area. Enjoy complimentary snacks and drinks to help your body recover. The staff will provide aftercare instructions. You will receive your compensation (gift card or other reward) before you leave.

Compensation & Rewards

The Lawton Blood Donor Center offers compensation to show appreciation for your time and contribution. While blood donation is fundamentally a lifesaving act of generosity, rewards help offset the time investment and encourage regular donations.

  • • Whole blood: $10-$20 in gift cards per donation
  • • Plasma: $25-$50+ per donation, with new donor bonuses often available
  • • Platelets: $20-$40 per donation, with loyalty bonuses for regular donors
  • • New donors: Many centers offer enhanced first-visit bonuses or welcome packages

For a detailed breakdown, see our Lawton Compensation Guide.

How Your Donation Helps Lawton

Every blood donation at the Lawton center has a direct, local impact. A single whole blood donation can save up to three lives by being separated into red blood cells, platelets, and plasma. Your donation may help:

🏥

Local Hospital Patients

Surgery patients, accident victims, and those with chronic conditions at Lawton-area hospitals.

🧬

Cancer Patients

Chemotherapy patients often need regular transfusions. Platelets are especially critical.

🚨

Emergency Response

Trauma patients and emergency situations in southwestern Oklahoma depend on a ready blood supply.

👶

Mothers & Newborns

Complications during childbirth can require emergency transfusions for both mother and baby.
