Donate Blood in Stillwater, Oklahoma — OSU & Community Donor Guide

Stillwater is home to Oklahoma State University and a strong community of blood donors. While there isn't a permanent donor center in Stillwater, Our Blood Institute hosts regular mobile blood drives on the OSU campus and at community locations throughout Payne County. Every unit donated stays in Oklahoma — serving local hospitals and trauma centers.

How Blood Donation Works in Stillwater

Because Stillwater does not have a permanent donor center, blood donations are collected through mobile drives hosted at community venues and on the Oklahoma State University campus. The process is the same as at any OBI center — just held at a temporary location.

Where to Find a Stillwater Blood Drive

  • OSU campus drives — held regularly at the Student Union, Colvin Recreation Center, and other campus venues throughout the semester
  • Community drives — hosted by local churches, businesses, and civic organizations in Stillwater and surrounding communities
  • Nearby donor centers — the closest permanent OBI centers are in Edmond, Enid, and Oklahoma City

To find the next drive near you, visit Our Blood Institute's drive schedule and enter your ZIP code.

What to Expect at a Mobile Blood Drive

  • Check-in and registration — bring your photo ID
  • Brief health screening and mini-physical
  • The blood draw — takes about 8 to 10 minutes
  • Rest and enjoy refreshments — plan about 45 to 60 minutes total

Eligibility Requirements

  • At least 17 years old (16 with parental consent)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in generally good health on donation day
  • Valid government-issued photo ID required
  • Wait at least 56 days between whole blood donations

For a complete list of eligibility criteria — including medications, travel history, and medical conditions — review the full OBI eligibility guide.
