B Negative Blood Type: Rare Donor Guide for Oklahoma

One of the rarest types — and among the most needed.

2%

of Oklahomans have B− blood

Your blood is critically needed at Oklahoma donor centers.

Quick Facts

Prevalence in Oklahoma ~2% of population
Can donate to B+, B−, AB+, AB−
Can receive from B− and O−
Most needed for Rh-negative B-type patients, specific surgical needs
Donation frequency Whole blood every 56 days
B negative (B−) is one of the rarest blood types in Oklahoma, found in only about 2% of the state's population. Despite its rarity, B− red blood cells can be donated to both Rh-positive and Rh-negative B and AB patients — four of the eight blood types. This versatility, combined with scarcity, makes B− donations exceptionally valuable.
For Rh-negative patients with B-type blood, B− is one of only two compatible options (the other being O−). Because Rh-negative patients cannot receive Rh-positive blood without serious immune risk, B− donors provide a critical — and hard-to-replace — resource. Hospitals often run B− shortages precisely because there are so few eligible donors.
B− donors can only receive from B− and O− — two of the rarest types — which further underscores the importance of giving regularly. OBI Blood Donor centers will often specifically reach out to B− donors during shortage periods. If you have B− blood, your commitment to regular donation directly determines whether B-negative patients receive compatible blood in time.

Common Myths About B- Blood

Myth: "B− is so rare that my donation barely helps the supply."

Fact: The opposite: your rarity is precisely why your donation matters so much. Each B− unit is essentially irreplaceable by any other type for Rh-negative B patients.

With only 2% of the population sharing your blood type, each B− donation is irreplaceable.
