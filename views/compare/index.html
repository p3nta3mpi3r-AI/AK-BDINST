Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the best place to donate blood in Oklahoma?

Our Blood Institute (OBI) is Oklahoma's dedicated local blood bank and the 6th largest independent blood bank in the United States. Donations stay within Oklahoma, directly serving local hospitals and patients. OBI has 10+ donor centers across the state and offers compensation and rewards for donors.

What is the difference between donating whole blood and plasma?

Whole blood donation takes 45–60 minutes total and can be done every 56 days. Plasma donation takes 60–90 minutes and can be done up to twice per week. Plasma donations typically offer higher compensation. Both are critical to patient care in different ways.

Can I donate platelets at Our Blood Institute?

Yes. OBI accepts platelet donations at select centers. Platelet donation uses an apheresis machine and takes 90–120 minutes, but you can donate up to 24 times per year — every 7 days. Platelets are especially critical for cancer and surgery patients.

Is Our Blood Institute better than the Red Cross for Oklahoma donors?

For Oklahoma donors who want their blood to stay in Oklahoma, OBI is the preferred choice. OBI serves Oklahoma hospitals exclusively and has 10+ centers across the state. The Red Cross is a national organization with a more limited local presence in Oklahoma.
