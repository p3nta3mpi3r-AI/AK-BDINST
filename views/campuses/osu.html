Where Drives Happen on Campus

Student Union Upper Plaza

The most frequent drive location. High foot traffic makes this a convenient spot for students between classes.

Willard Hall (ROTC Building)

Regular drives serving the engineering and ROTC community on campus.

Kappa Alpha Chapter

Greek chapter-hosted drives open to all OSU students and community members.

Delta Tau Delta & Farmhouse

Additional Greek chapter drives hosted throughout the semester at their respective chapter houses.

How to Sign Up

Signing up for an OSU blood drive takes less than 2 minutes. Use the Donable platform to choose a convenient time slot at your preferred campus location. You can also contact the OSU drive coordinator directly at brandi.atkinson@obi.org or call 580-231-3047 to inquire about upcoming drive dates.

What to Expect as a Student Donor

Most OSU campus blood drives take about 45 minutes from start to finish. Here's what the process looks like:

  1. Check-in: Present your photo ID and complete a brief health questionnaire.
  2. Mini physical: Staff will check your blood pressure, pulse, temperature, and hemoglobin level.
  3. Donation: The actual blood draw takes 8–10 minutes for whole blood.
  4. Recovery: Spend 10–15 minutes in the refreshment area with snacks and drinks provided by OBI.

Eligibility Basics

  • At least 17 years old (no parental consent needed at 17+)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in generally good health on donation day
  • Bring a valid photo ID (student ID accepted)

Not sure if you qualify? Check our full eligibility requirements or review the donation frequency guide.

OSU Blood Drive FAQs

Where do OSU blood drives take place on campus?
OSU blood drives are held at the Student Union Upper Plaza, Willard Hall (ROTC building), and through Greek chapter houses including Kappa Alpha, Delta Tau Delta, and Farmhouse. Locations vary by semester.
How do I sign up for the OSU blood drive?
Use the Donable scheduling platform to book your appointment. You can also contact the OSU drive coordinator at brandi.atkinson@obi.org or 580-231-3047. Walk-ins are also welcome at most drives.
Do I need to be an OSU student to donate?
No. Campus drives are organized primarily for the OSU community, but any eligible donor in the Stillwater area is welcome. You must be at least 17 years old, weigh at least 110 lbs, and be in generally good health.
How often does OBI host drives at OSU?
Our Blood Institute hosts drives at OSU multiple times per semester — typically every 4 to 8 weeks during the fall and spring academic terms. Greek chapters also organize their own drives throughout the year.
What should I bring to the OSU blood drive?
Bring a valid photo ID (student ID accepted), eat a good meal beforehand, and drink plenty of water. Wear a shirt with sleeves that roll up easily. The entire process takes about 45 minutes.
