Everything you need to know about donating blood and plasma in Stillwater, OK (74074). Eligibility, what to expect, preparation tips, and how to schedule your appointment.

Eligibility Requirements

To donate blood or plasma in Stillwater, you must meet these basic requirements:

  • ✅ Age: 17 or older (16 with parental consent in Oklahoma). Plasma donors must be 18+.
  • ✅ Weight: At least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • ✅ Health: Generally healthy and feeling well on donation day
  • ✅ ID: Valid government-issued photo ID required
  • ✅ Tattoos: 3-month wait after tattoo at a state-regulated facility in Oklahoma
  • ✅ Medications: Most common medications are fine. Antibiotics may require a short deferral.

The Donation Process at a Stillwater Mobile Drive

Mobile blood drives follow the same clinical protocol as permanent centers. Here's what happens from check-in to checkout:

  1. Check-in (5 minutes): Present your photo ID and complete a brief registration. If you pre-scheduled online, your information is already in the system.
  2. Health Screening (10–15 minutes): A staff member will review your health history, check your temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and take a small finger-prick sample to check your hemoglobin level.
  3. The Donation (8–10 minutes): You'll be seated in a comfortable chair. The phlebotomist will clean your arm, insert a sterile needle, and collect one unit (about 1 pint) of blood. You can squeeze a stress ball to help the flow.
  4. Rest & Refreshments (10–15 minutes): After the needle is removed, you'll rest in a recovery area with snacks and drinks — typically juice, water, and cookies. This step is important to prevent lightheadedness.
  5. Checkout (2 minutes): Staff will provide post-donation care instructions and a thank-you gift.

Local Relevance: Why Stillwater & OSU Matter for Blood Donation

Stillwater plays a unique role in Oklahoma's blood donation ecosystem. As the home of Oklahoma State University — the state's second-largest university — the city has a concentrated population of young, healthy potential donors at a critical time when the national blood supply faces chronic shortages.

OSU's campus blood drives are particularly important because:

  • Young donor pool: OSU's student body of over 25,000 represents one of the largest concentrations of eligible donors in north-central Oklahoma.
  • Type O need: The student population helps supply O-negative and O-positive units, which are the most critical for trauma care at Stillwater Medical Center and regional hospitals.
  • Community impact: Every unit donated at a Stillwater drive stays in Oklahoma — helping patients at Stillwater Medical Center, OU Medical Center, and other Oklahoma hospitals.

Our Blood Institute is Oklahoma's independent nonprofit blood center, serving over 80 hospitals statewide. Donations collected in Stillwater directly support your neighbors, classmates, and community.

Common Questions — Blood Donation in Stillwater

Bring a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport), and be prepared for a brief health screening. First-time plasma donors also need proof of Social Security number and current address.
