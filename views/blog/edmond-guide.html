Eligibility Requirements

To donate blood or plasma in Edmond, you must meet these basic requirements:

  • ✅ Age: 17 or older (16 with parental consent in Oklahoma). Plasma donors must be 18+.
  • ✅ Weight: At least 110 pounds (50 kg)
  • ✅ Health: Generally healthy and feeling well on donation day
  • ✅ ID: Valid government-issued photo ID required
  • ✅ Tattoos: 3-month wait after tattoo at a state-regulated facility in Oklahoma
  • ✅ Medications: Most common medications are fine. Antibiotics may require a short deferral.

Step-by-Step: Your Donation Visit

Here is exactly what to expect when you visit the Edmond Blood Donor Center at 721 S Broadway:

1

Schedule Your Appointment

Book online at obi.org or call (405) 509-4700. Walk-ins are welcome at the Edmond center, but appointments get priority and reduce your wait time. Choose a time when you are well-rested and well-hydrated.

2

Check In & Registration

Arrive at 721 S Broadway with your government-issued photo ID. First-time donors will complete a registration form with basic personal and health information. Returning donors can check in quickly with their donor ID.

3

Health Screening

A staff member will check your temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin levels. You will answer a confidential health questionnaire. This screening takes about 10-15 minutes and ensures your safety during the donation.

4

The Donation

You will recline in a comfortable chair while a trained phlebotomist draws your blood. Whole blood takes about 8-10 minutes. Plasma and platelet donations take longer (45 min to 2.5 hours) as blood is processed through an apheresis machine. Most donors read, watch TV, or browse their phone during the draw.

5

Rest & Refreshments

After your donation, relax for 10-15 minutes in the refreshment area. Enjoy complimentary snacks and drinks to help your body recover. The staff will provide aftercare instructions. You will receive a thank-you gift before you leave.
