Types of Blood Donations Available in Oklahoma City

1. Whole Blood Donation

Whole blood donation is the most common type. It collects all components of your blood in a single draw.

  • Takes approximately 8–10 minutes for the actual draw
  • Can be donated every 56 days (8 weeks)
  • Typically compensated with gift cards or rewards points
  • One donation can save up to three lives

2. Plasma Donation

Plasma is the liquid portion of your blood, rich in proteins and antibodies. It's collected using a special apheresis machine.

  • Takes 60–90 minutes per session
  • Can be donated twice per week (at least 48 hours apart)
  • Longer session time; appointment recommended
  • Used to create life-saving medications

3. Platelet Donation

Platelets are tiny cell fragments that help blood clot. Platelet donations are especially critical for cancer patients undergoing chemotherapy.

  • Takes 90–120 minutes
  • Can be donated every 7 days, up to 24 times per year
  • Critical for cancer patients and transplant recipients

Step-by-Step: Your First Donation at the Oklahoma City Center

Step 1 — Schedule Your Appointment

While walk-ins are welcome, scheduling an appointment guarantees your spot and minimizes wait time. You can book online or call the center at (405) 278-3100. The Central OKC Blood Donor Center is conveniently located at 901 N. Lincoln, Oklahoma City, OK 73104.

Step 2 — Prepare for Your Donation

  • Drink at least 16 oz of water before your appointment
  • Eat a healthy, iron-rich meal within 3 hours of donating
  • Avoid fatty foods if donating plasma
  • Get a full night of sleep the night before
  • Wear comfortable clothing with sleeves that roll up easily
  • Bring a valid photo ID and a list of current medications

Step 3 — Registration & Health Screening (15–20 min)

When you arrive, check in at the front desk with your ID. Staff will walk you through a health history questionnaire, check your vital signs, and perform a finger-stick hemoglobin test.

Step 4 — The Donation (8–90 min depending on type)

You'll be seated in a comfortable donation chair. A trained phlebotomist will clean your arm and insert a sterile, single-use needle. Most donors report only a brief pinch.

Step 5 — Recovery & Compensation (10–15 min)

After your donation, spend a few minutes in the recovery area. Enjoy complimentary snacks and beverages, receive post-donation care instructions, and Collect your thank-you gift.

What Happens During Your Donation

  1. 1. Check-in (5 min): Arrive at the center, present your ID, and confirm your appointment or sign in as a walk-in.
  2. 2. Health Screening (10–15 min): Answer a confidential health questionnaire. A technician checks your temperature, blood pressure, pulse, and hemoglobin/hematocrit levels via a quick finger prick.
  3. 3. The Donation:
    • • Whole blood: A single needle draw lasting about 8–10 minutes
    • • Plasma: An apheresis machine separates plasma and returns red blood cells — 45–90 minutes
    • • Platelets: Similar apheresis process, 1.5–2.5 hours
  4. 4. Recovery (10–15 min): Rest in the recovery area, enjoy a snack and beverage, and make sure you feel well before leaving.

Common Questions — Blood Donation in Oklahoma City

Bring a valid government-issued photo ID (driver's license or passport), and be prepared for a brief health screening. First-time plasma donors also need proof of Social Security number and current address.
