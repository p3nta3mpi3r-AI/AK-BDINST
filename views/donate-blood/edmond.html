Edmond has transformed from a small suburb into one of Oklahoma's most populated cities, with over 100,000 residents and a major university — the University of Central Oklahoma — at its center. The Edmond Blood Donor Center at 3409 S. Broadway, Suite 300 serves the entire northern OKC corridor, including Arcadia, Deer Creek, Piedmont, and Guthrie. For UCO students, the center is close to campus and open on weekdays and weekends.

How do I donate blood in Edmond, Oklahoma?

Edmond donors directly support patients at Mercy Hospital Edmond, INTEGRIS Health Edmond, and the broader Oklahoma City hospital network. Because Edmond's population skews younger and healthier than the state average — driven in part by the student and professional population — it's one of the more important donor markets in the metro area. High donor quality translates to higher-value donations for patients.

What should I bring to donate blood in Edmond?

UCO students looking to donate should bring their student ID as supplemental identification (a government-issued photo ID is still required). The donation process takes about 45–60 minutes for whole blood and does not typically interfere with a normal class schedule if you plan for a late-morning or early-afternoon slot. Plasma donors, who give up to twice weekly, often prefer the higher compensation and find the longer appointment time manageable between or after classes.
