Yukon sits along the historic Route 66 corridor in the western Oklahoma City metro, a community that has grown steadily as the OKC region expands westward. The Yukon Blood Donor Center at 4312 Grant Boulevard serves Yukon and the surrounding communities of Mustang, El Reno, Piedmont, and Bethany — a corridor of suburban growth where many newer residents have not yet established a local donation routine.

How do I donate blood in Yukon, Oklahoma?

The Yukon center is a convenient alternative for donors who live on the western side of the OKC metro and want to avoid driving downtown. With weekday evening and weekend hours, it accommodates working adults who donate outside of business hours. First-time donors from Yukon, Mustang, and El Reno are especially encouraged to schedule a visit — these communities have strong populations of eligible donors who are underrepresented in the current donor pool relative to the metro average.

What should I bring to donate blood in Yukon?

Blood donated at the Yukon center enters the same regional supply network as donations from Oklahoma City, Norman, and Edmond, and goes to whichever OKC-area hospital has the most urgent need. The western OKC corridor has seen significant population growth over the past decade — and blood supply infrastructure scales with community participation, not automatically with population. More Yukon-area donors mean better preparedness for the metro-wide patient population.

Blood Donation Questions for Yukon, OK

The Yukon Blood Donor Center is at 4312 Grant Boulevard, Yukon, OK 73099. It serves Yukon, Mustang, El Reno, Piedmont, and Bethany.

The Yukon center is open Monday–Friday 7:00 AM–7:00 PM and Saturday–Sunday 8:00 AM–5:00 PM. Hours may vary on holidays — call ahead or check the Our Blood Institute website.

Donations from Yukon enter the central Oklahoma blood supply network and go to OKC-area hospitals based on patient need. Your donation could help a trauma patient, cancer patient, or surgical patient anywhere in the metro.

Promotions vary and are updated regularly. Check with the Yukon center directly or visit the Our Blood Institute website for current new donor offers and compensation rates.
