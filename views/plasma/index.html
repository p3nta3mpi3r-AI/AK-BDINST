About Plasma Donation in Oklahoma

Oklahoma is home to a growing network of plasma donation centers serving communities from Oklahoma City and Tulsa to smaller cities like Ada, Enid, and Stillwater. Plasma — the liquid component of blood — is essential for manufacturing therapies that treat immune deficiencies, bleeding disorders, burns, and other life-threatening conditions.

When you donate plasma in Oklahoma, you’re contributing to a national supply chain that helps tens of thousands of patients annually. The process is safe, FDA-regulated, and typically takes 60–90 minutes for established donors. Most centers compensate donors for their time, and new donors often receive promotional bonuses during their first several visits.

Quick Facts About Plasma Donation

  • Frequency: Up to twice per week with 48 hours between donations
  • Duration: 60–90 minutes for return visits; 2–2.5 hours for first-timers
  • Age: 18–69 years old (some locations accept 17 with consent)
  • Weight: Minimum 110 pounds
  • Recovery: Your body replaces donated plasma within 24–48 hours
  • Compensation: Paid onto prepaid debit card after each visit
