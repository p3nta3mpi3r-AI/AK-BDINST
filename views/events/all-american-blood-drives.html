Everything You Need to Know About the All American Blood Drives

The All American Blood Drives are OBI Blood Donor’s largest annual summer campaign, held every year across Oklahoma, Texas, and Arkansas. This patriotic celebration of giving brings blood donation directly into communities — from OKC malls and Tulsa stadiums to small-town fire stations and military bases.

Why Oklahoma Needs Your Blood Every Summer

Every two seconds, someone in the United States needs a blood transfusion. Oklahoma’s hospitals require 800+ units of blood every single day to serve trauma patients, surgical cases, cancer treatments, and chronic conditions. Summer is historically the most challenging season for blood banks — school’s out, families travel, and regular donors miss their appointments.

The All American Blood Drives are specifically designed to bridge this critical summer gap. With community events across three states, OBI Blood Donor collects thousands of life-saving units during the period when supply dips lowest.

How It Works

  1. Find a drive near you — browse the schedule below or book through the scheduling form
  2. Show up or walk in — appointments are encouraged but never required
  3. Complete a mini-physical — quick pulse, blood pressure, temperature, and hemoglobin check (5 minutes)
  4. Donate blood — the actual draw takes just 8–10 minutes
  5. Enjoy your snacks and gifts — rest for 10–15 minutes, grab refreshments, and pick up your free t-shirt, camp chair, and theme park voucher

One Donation = Three Lives Saved

Your single pint of whole blood is separated into three components — red blood cells (for trauma and surgery), platelets (for cancer patients), and plasma (for burn victims and bleeding disorders). Each goes to a different patient. That means one donation can directly impact three families in Oklahoma hospitals.

Blood donated at any Oklahoma All American Blood Drive stays local. It goes directly to hospitals across Oklahoma, Arkansas, and Texas — serving your neighbors, coworkers, and community members.

Eligibility at a Glance

  • Must be at least 16 years old (parental consent required for age 16)
  • Weigh at least 110 pounds
  • Be in general good health and feeling well on the day of donation
  • Have not donated whole blood in the past 56 days
  • Have a valid photo ID

Not sure if you qualify? Call OBI Blood Donor at 📅 Walk-in Info or check our full eligibility requirements.

Tips for a Great Donation Experience

  • Eat a healthy, iron-rich meal 2–3 hours before (spinach, beans, lean meat)
  • Drink plenty of water — aim for 16 oz extra the morning of your appointment
  • Avoid fatty foods (they can interfere with blood testing)
  • Get a full night’s sleep the night before
  • Wear a shirt with sleeves you can roll up easily
  • Bring your photo ID and donor card if you have one

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the All American Blood Drives?
The All American Blood Drives are a special summer blood drive campaign organized by OBI Blood Donor. Running from June 11 to July 5, 2026, these patriotic events encourage Americans to donate blood at convenient community locations across Oklahoma, Texas, and Arkansas. Every donor receives a free All American Blood Donor t-shirt, a folding camp chair, and a theme park voucher while supplies last.
Who can donate blood at these events?
Most healthy adults aged 16 and older (with parental consent for 16-year-olds) who weigh at least 110 pounds can donate. You must feel well on the day of donation. There is no upper age limit. Certain medications, recent tattoos (within 3 months in some states), and travel to specific countries may affect eligibility. If you're unsure, visit oklahomabloodinstitute.com for details.
Do I need an appointment to donate?
No — walk-ins are welcome at every All American Blood Drive location. However, making an appointment helps reduce wait times and ensures staffing is available. You can book by calling or clicking the 'Book' button next to your preferred location on this page.
How long does a blood donation take?
The actual blood draw takes about 8 to 10 minutes. Including the mini-physical, paperwork, and rest period afterward, plan for about 45 minutes to 1 hour total. First-time donors may take slightly longer.
What do I get for donating?
While supplies last, every successful donor at an All American Blood Drive receives: (1) a limited-edition All American Blood Donor t-shirt, (2) a folding camp chair, and (3) a voucher for a free ticket to a local theme park or attraction. Check the voucher details for participating attractions near you.
How many lives can my donation save?
A single whole-blood donation can save up to three lives. Your blood is separated into red blood cells, platelets, and plasma — each component goes to a different patient. Blood donated at Oklahoma drives stays local, serving hospitals in Oklahoma, Arkansas, and Texas.
What should I eat or drink before donating?
Eat a healthy meal and drink plenty of water in the hours before your donation. Avoid fatty foods (they can affect blood testing), and stay hydrated. A good breakfast or lunch with iron-rich foods like spinach, beans, or lean meat is ideal. Avoid alcohol for at least 24 hours before donating.
Can I donate if I have a tattoo?
In Oklahoma, you can donate blood 3 months after getting a tattoo if it was done at a state-regulated facility. If the tattoo was done in another state or at an unregulated location, a 3-month deferral may apply. Visit oklahomabloodinstitute.com for specific guidance.
